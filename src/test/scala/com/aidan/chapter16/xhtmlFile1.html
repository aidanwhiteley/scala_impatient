Downloads!

In a slight attempt to save some ISP bandwidth, the links to music files are password protected. The userid is "CriticalError" (case sensitive) and the password is my dog's name (which can be easily found elsewhere on this site - all lowercase).

This song (mp3 format or iTunes m4a format) is from a later tape where all sorts of bits and pieces were recorded on a ghetto blaster. Rose's voice is really great on this (I'm fairly sure this is just a recording of a practice at someone's house) and I love the DADGAD guitar tuning. While the acoustic guitar "booms" away at the low notes, I'm still pretty pleased with the "middle eight" guitar piece in this - I still think it is one of the better bits I've written on guitar.

This song (mp3 format) was recorded direct from the mixing desk at a gig at The Sleeper in Seaton. I'm guessing it is from about 1988 when Critical Error were gigging fairly frequently. The reason for picking this one out was that my band mates were daft generous enough to let me play lead, acoustic guitar all over the song. Well you don't turn down opportunities like that so I did with an enthusiasm completely unmatched by my level of musical skill. Still - great fun!

This song (mp3 format or iTunes m4a format) is from an early demo with Critical Error. What I like about this song is that it reminds me how much I used to enjoy playing guitar with Steve Mavin. The other memory from recording this is that we were using a friend's (Baz Bishop) Boss Dimension C guitar pedal. All the synth sounding background "swells" as the song progresses are just guitars, basses and vocals going via the aformentioned Dimension C pedal.
